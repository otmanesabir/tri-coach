# Scientific References

Every workout template and zone calculation in tri-coach is backed by peer-reviewed research, textbooks, and established coaching methodologies. This document collects all citations used across the codebase.

---

## Textbooks & Coaching Frameworks

- **Daniels, J. (2014).** *Daniels' Running Formula*, 3rd ed. Human Kinetics.
  — VDOT tables, pace zones (E/M/T/I/R), interval prescription. The gold standard for running training intensity.
- **Daniels, J. & Gilbert, J. (1979).** *Oxygen Power*. Published tables.
  — Original VDOT oxygen cost tables underlying the Daniels pace calculator.
- **Friel, J. (2009).** *The Triathlete's Training Bible*, 3rd ed. VeloPress.
  — 7-zone HR system, periodization for multisport, field testing protocols.
- **Allen, H. & Coggan, A. (2010).** *Training and Racing with a Power Meter*, 2nd ed. VeloPress.
  — FTP-based power zones, TSS, IF, NP definitions. Standard cycling power model.
- **Maglischo, E.W. (2003).** *Swimming Fastest*. Human Kinetics.
  — Comprehensive swim training science, energy systems, technique analysis.
- **Laughlin, T. (2004).** *Total Immersion: The Revolutionary Way to Swim Better, Faster, and Easier*. Touchstone.
  — Technique-first swim methodology, drill progressions for adult learners.

## Training Intensity & Polarized Training

- **Seiler, S. (2010).** What is best practice for training intensity and duration distribution in endurance athletes? *Int J Sports Physiol Perform*, 5(3), 276-291.
  — Foundational paper on 80/20 polarized training distribution.
- **Seiler, S. & Kjerland, G.Ø. (2006).** Quantifying training intensity distribution in elite endurance athletes. *Scand J Med Sci Sports*, 16(1), 49-56.
  — Evidence that elite athletes spend ~75-80% of training below lactate threshold.
- **Esteve-Lanao, J. et al. (2007).** How do endurance runners actually train? Relationship with competition performance. *Med Sci Sports Exerc*, 39(3), 496-504.
  — Confirms polarized distribution correlates with better race performance.
- **Buchheit, M. & Laursen, P.B. (2013).** High-intensity interval training, solutions to the programming puzzle. *Sports Med*, 43, 313-338.
  — Comprehensive HIIT programming framework.

---

## Lactate Threshold & Tempo Training

- **Faude, O. et al. (2009).** Lactate threshold concepts: how valid are they? *Sports Med*, 39(6), 469-490.
  — Critical review of LT concepts and their validity for training prescription.
- **Billat, V.L. et al. (2003).** The concept of maximal lactate steady state. *Sports Med*, 33(6), 407-426.
  — Defines MLSS and its relationship to threshold training intensity.
- **Billat, V.L. (2001).** Interval training for performance: a scientific and empirical practice. *Sports Med*, 31(2), 75-90.
  — Scientific basis for interval training across endurance sports.

---

## VO2max & Interval Training

- **Midgley, A.W. et al. (2006).** Is there an optimal training intensity for enhancing VO2max? *Sports Med*, 36(2), 117-132.
  — Evidence that 95-100% VO2max is optimal for VO2max development.
- **Midgley, A.W. et al. (2007).** Training to enhance the physiological determinants of long-distance running performance. *Sports Med*, 37(10), 857-880.
  — Comprehensive review of training methods for distance running.
- **Bacon, A.P. et al. (2013).** VO2max trainability and high intensity interval training in humans. *PLoS One*, 8(9), e73182.
  — HIIT as the most effective stimulus for VO2max improvement.
- **Laursen, P.B. & Jenkins, D.G. (2002).** The scientific basis for high-intensity interval training. *Sports Med*, 32(1), 53-73.
  — Foundational review of HIIT physiology and prescription.

## Running Economy & Biomechanics

- **Barnes, K.R. & Kilding, A.E. (2015).** Running economy: measurement, norms, and determining factors. *Sports Med Open*, 1, 8.
  — Comprehensive review of factors affecting running economy.
- **Paavolainen, L. et al. (1999).** Explosive-strength training improves 5-km running time by improving running economy and muscle power. *J Appl Physiol*, 86(5), 1527-1533.
  — Landmark study showing plyometrics improve running economy by 8%.
- **Saunders, P.U. et al. (2006).** Short-term plyometric training improves running economy in highly trained middle and long distance runners. *J Strength Cond Res*, 20(4), 947-954.
  — Confirms plyometric benefits for already-trained runners.
- **Spurrs, R.W. et al. (2003).** The effect of plyometric training on distance running performance. *Eur J Appl Physiol*, 89, 1-7.
  — 6 weeks of plyometrics improved 3km time by 2.7%.

## Strength Training for Endurance Athletes

- **Rønnestad, B.R. & Mujika, I. (2014).** Optimizing strength training for running and cycling endurance performance. *Scand J Med Sci Sports*, 24(4), 603-612.
  — Key review: heavy strength (3-6 reps) improves economy without hypertrophy.
- **Beattie, K. et al. (2014).** The effect of strength training on performance in endurance athletes. *Sports Med*, 44(6), 845-865.
  — Meta-analysis showing 2-8% improvement in running economy from strength training.
- **Støren, Ø. et al. (2008).** Maximal strength training improves running economy. *Med Sci Sports Exerc*, 40(6), 1087-1092.
  — 4 × 4RM half-squats improved running economy by 5% in 8 weeks.
- **Schoenfeld, B.J. et al. (2015).** Influence of resistance training frequency on muscular adaptations in well-trained men. *J Strength Cond Res*, 29(7), 1821-1829.
  — Training frequency guidelines for strength programming.

---

## Injury Prevention & Core Stability

- **Lauersen, J.B. et al. (2014).** The effectiveness of exercise interventions to prevent sports injuries. *Br J Sports Med*, 48(11), 871-877.
  — Strength training reduces injuries by ~70%. The strongest evidence for injury prevention.
- **Fredericson, M. & Moore, T. (2005).** Muscular balance, core stability, and injury prevention for middle and long-distance runners. *Phys Med Rehabil Clin*, 16(3), 669-689.
  — Core stability protocols for runners.
- **Sato, K. & Mokha, M. (2009).** Does core strength training influence running kinetics? *J Strength Cond Res*, 23(1), 133-140.
  — Core training improves running mechanics.
- **Willy, R.W. & Davis, I.S. (2011).** The effect of a hip-strengthening program on mechanics during running and during a single-leg squat. *J Orthop Sports Phys Ther*, 41(9), 625-632.
  — Hip strengthening improves running mechanics and reduces injury risk.

## Heart Rate Zone Methodology

- **Karvonen, M.J. et al. (1957).** The effects of training on heart rate. *Ann Med Exp Biol Fenn*, 35, 307-315.
  — Original heart rate reserve formula: THR = ((HRmax − HRrest) × %intensity) + HRrest.
- **ACSM (2011).** Quantity and quality of exercise for developing and maintaining fitness in apparently healthy adults. *Med Sci Sports Exerc*, 43(7), 1334-1359.
  — Guidelines for exercise prescription across populations.

---

## Aerobic Base & Fat Oxidation

- **Holloszy, J.O. & Coyle, E.F. (1984).** Adaptations of skeletal muscle to endurance exercise. *J Appl Physiol*, 56(4), 831-838.
  — Foundational paper on mitochondrial adaptations from Zone 2 training.
- **Brooks, G.A. & Mercier, J. (1994).** Balance of carbohydrate and lipid utilization during exercise: the "crossover" concept. *J Appl Physiol*, 76(6), 2253-2261.
  — The crossover concept explaining fat vs. carb usage at different intensities.

## Recovery Science

- **Menzies, P. et al. (2010).** Blood lactate clearance during active recovery after an intense running bout depends on the intensity of active recovery. *J Sports Sci*, 28(9), 975-982.
  — Active recovery at low intensity clears lactate faster than passive rest.
- **Dupuy, O. et al. (2018).** An evidence-based approach for choosing post-exercise recovery techniques. *Front Physiol*, 9, 403.
  — Systematic review of recovery modalities and their effectiveness.

## Cycling & Power Training

- **Hawley, J.A. et al. (1997).** Training techniques to improve fatigue resistance and enhance endurance performance. *J Sports Sci*, 15(3), 325-333.
  — Endurance training principles applied to cycling.
- **Hawley, J.A. & Burke, L.M. (2010).** Carbohydrate availability and training adaptation. *Exerc Sport Sci Rev*, 38(4), 152-160.
  — Nutrition periodization for endurance performance.

## Swimming Science

- **Toussaint, H.M. & Beek, P.J. (1992).** Biomechanics of competitive front crawl swimming. *Sports Med*, 13(1), 8-24.
  — Foundational swim biomechanics research.
- **Wakayoshi, K. et al. (1992).** Determination of critical power and critical stroke rate in front crawl swimming. *Eur J Appl Physiol*, 64, 419-424.
  — Original Critical Swim Speed (CSS) methodology.
- **Dekerle, J. et al. (2002).** Validity and reliability of critical speed in relation to swimming performance. *Int J Sports Med*, 23(2), 93-98.
  — Validates CSS as a training intensity anchor for swimmers.
- **Aspenes, S.T. & Karlsen, T. (2012).** Exercise-training intervention studies in competitive swimming. *Sports Med*, 42(6), 527-543.
  — Review of evidence-based swim training interventions.
- **Crowley, E. et al. (2017).** The impact of resistance training on swimming performance. *Sports Med*, 47(11), 2285-2307.
  — Dry-land strength training for swimmers.

## Triathlon-Specific

- **Bentley, D.J. et al. (2007).** Specific aspects of contemporary triathlon. *Sports Med*, 37(4-5), 247-267.
  — Comprehensive review of triathlon physiology and training.
- **Millet, G.P. & Vleck, V.E. (2000).** Physiological and biomechanical adaptations to the cycle-to-run transition in Olympic triathlon. *Sports Med*, 30(6), 583-599.
  — The science of bike-to-run transitions (brick training rationale).
- **Hue, O. et al. (1998).** The effect of prior cycling on biomechanical and metabolic variables during running in triathletes. *Med Sci Sports Exerc*, 30(7), 1297-1302.
  — Why running off the bike feels different and how to train for it.

## Hill Training

- **Ferley, D.D. et al. (2014).** Incline treadmill interval training: its role in improving running economy. *J Strength Cond Res*, 28(6), 1475-1483.
  — Hill repeats improve running economy by 2-3%.
