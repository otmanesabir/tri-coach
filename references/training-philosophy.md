# Training Philosophy - Half-Distance Triathlon

## Challenge Almere-Amsterdam 2026 Context

**Event**: September 12, 2026
**Distance**: Half-distance triathlon (1.9km swim, 90km bike, 21.1km run)  
**Target**: Sub-5:30 finish time
**Timeline**: ~18 months of preparation

## Periodization Framework

### Base Phase (Current - 6 months out)
- **Focus**: Aerobic base building, movement efficiency
- **Volume**: 70% of training in Zone 1-2
- **Key Sessions**: 
  - Long easy runs (60-90min)
  - Steady bike rides (90-150min)
  - Technique-focused swimming
- **Recovery**: High priority - aim for >60% recovery scores

### Build Phase (6-3 months out)  
- **Focus**: Race-specific fitness, lactate threshold
- **Volume**: 60% Zone 1-2, 30% Zone 3-4, 10% Zone 5
- **Key Sessions**:
  - Tempo runs (20-40min at threshold)
  - Sweet spot bike intervals
  - CSS swimming intervals
- **Recovery**: Monitor closely - 50-60% acceptable during build blocks

### Peak Phase (3 months - race)
- **Focus**: Race simulation, speed work
- **Volume**: Maintain base, add race-pace efforts  
- **Key Sessions**:
  - Brick workouts (bike-run)
  - Race-pace intervals
  - Open water swimming
- **Recovery**: Taper priorities - return to 65%+ scores

## Training Zones (Heart Rate)

### Running (Apple Watch data)
- **Zone 1**: Easy conversational pace (~70-80% HRmax)
- **Zone 2**: Comfortable aerobic (~80-87% HRmax)
- **Zone 3**: Tempo/threshold (~87-93% HRmax)  
- **Zone 4**: Lactate intervals (~93-97% HRmax)
- **Zone 5**: Neuromuscular power (>97% HRmax)

### Cycling
- **Zone 1-2**: Base endurance (RPE 1-4)
- **Zone 3**: Sweet spot/tempo (RPE 5-6)
- **Zone 4**: Threshold/FTP (RPE 7-8)
- **Zone 5**: VO2max (RPE 9-10)

## Recovery-Based Training Decisions

### Green Recovery (>66%)
- **Go**: High-intensity sessions, long workouts
- **Example**: "Great recovery score - perfect day for those 5x1k intervals"

### Yellow Recovery (33-66%) 
- **Caution**: Moderate intensity, shorter duration
- **Example**: "Recovery is moderate - let's do that tempo run but cut it to 20min"

### Red Recovery (<33%)
- **Rest**: Easy sessions only, consider day off
- **Example**: "Low recovery today - easy 30min jog or rest day"

## Weekly Structure Template

### High Volume Weeks
- **Monday**: Rest or easy swim
- **Tuesday**: Quality run (intervals/tempo)  
- **Wednesday**: Moderate bike + easy run (brick)
- **Thursday**: Quality swim + easy run
- **Friday**: Rest or easy bike
- **Saturday**: Long bike ride
- **Sunday**: Long run

### Recovery Weeks (every 4th week)
- Reduce volume by 30-40%
- Maintain intensity but shorter duration
- Extra rest day
- Focus on sleep and nutrition

## Key Performance Indicators

### Training Load Management
- **Weekly TSS**: 400-600 (build), 300-400 (base)
- **Acute:Chronic Ratio**: Keep between 0.8-1.3
- **Strain Score**: Target 12-16 per day average

### Recovery Metrics  
- **HRV Trend**: Rising or stable over weeks
- **Resting HR**: Within 5bpm of baseline
- **Sleep Efficiency**: >85% most nights
- **Recovery Score**: >50% average across week

## Coaching Decision Tree

### High-Intensity Day Planning
1. Check recovery score
2. Assess recent training load
3. Review HRV trend
4. Consider sleep quality
5. Adjust or proceed

### Red Flags for Overreaching
- Recovery <33% for 3+ consecutive days
- Resting HR elevated >10bpm from baseline  
- HRV declining for >5 days
- Poor sleep efficiency (<80%) multiple nights
- Subjective fatigue despite adequate rest

## Race-Specific Considerations

### Swim (1.9km)
- **Target**: 35-40 minutes
- **Focus**: Endurance, open water skills, race tactics
- **Key**: Consistent stroke rate, bilateral breathing

### Bike (90km)  
- **Target**: 2:30-2:45 hours
- **Focus**: Sustained power, aerodynamics, nutrition
- **Key**: 70.3 race pace ~80-85% of FTP

### Run (21.1km)
- **Target**: 1:45-2:00 hours  
- **Focus**: Run off the bike, negative split strategy
- **Key**: Start conservative, build through middle 10k

## Environmental Adaptations

### Weather Considerations
- **Cold water**: Wetsuit practice, breathing drills
- **Heat**: Hydration strategies, heat acclimatization  
- **Wind**: Bike handling skills, pacing adjustments

### Equipment
- **No power meter**: Use heart rate and perceived effort
- **Apple Watch**: Leverage GPS splits and HR data for pacing
- **Race gear**: Practice with exact race-day setup
